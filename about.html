About the Platform

An authority platform for serious AI thinking.

First Principles AI is designed as an independent editorial and advisory platform focused on four connected domains: frontier AI research, real AI systems, job architecture under AI, and theory transfer from mathematics, physics, and systems disciplines into AI.

Positioning

Why this platform exists

Most AI discourse breaks down into one of two extremes: shallow trend commentary or narrow technical specialization disconnected from strategic reality. This platform is designed to sit in the more difficult middle: rigorous enough to matter, clear enough to travel, and practical enough to inform real decisions.

The purpose is not just to comment on AI, but to build a durable body of work that translates technical change into operational, institutional, and economic understanding.

Core Identity
  • First-principles interpretation of frontier AI research
  • System-level analysis of automation and applied AI workflows
  • Task-based understanding of how jobs and work structures change under AI
  • Original frameworks that combine AI with deeper theories from math, physics, engineering, and systems science

For technical readers

The platform explains architectures, research developments, and system design with enough structure to be useful to engineers, analysts, and technically ambitious readers.

For operators and strategists

The work connects AI capability to workflows, organizational design, job transformation, and implementation constraints across real industries.

For institutions and expert forums

The site is designed to support briefings, advisory work, expert network conversations, and participation in serious policy or strategy discussions.

Editorial Philosophy

The aim is synthesis with consequence.

Understanding AI is not only about knowing what a model can do. It is about understanding what changes when technical capability meets work, systems, incentives, institutions, and the structure of the world itself.

That is why the site is built around four major domains rather than one narrow niche. The boundaries between research, systems, labor, and theory are exactly where the most important long-term questions now sit.

As the platform grows, it is meant to support a body of work that is simultaneously readable, rigorous, visually strong, and credible in advisory contexts.

Use of the Platform

This site is designed to support publishing, advisory work, and expert positioning.

Over time, the platform can support long-form essays, research briefings, speaking invitations, boutique consulting, expert-network engagements, and institutional collaborations across Europe and beyond.
