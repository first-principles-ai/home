03 · Job Architecture under AI

Work is being restructured task by task.

This section analyzes how AI changes roles by decomposing them into tasks, decisions, interfaces, and skill structures. The focus is not on vague replacement narratives, but on the architecture of work itself.

Role Breakdowns Task Architecture Augmentation vs Automation Future Skills
Role Breakdowns

Job profiles should be analyzed as systems.

Roles are usually discussed at the wrong level of abstraction. AI rarely replaces an entire job in one move. It changes combinations of tasks, compresses some forms of labor, raises the value of others, and shifts how decisions are made and by whom.

Example target roles
  • Consultant
  • Supply chain planner
  • Finance analyst
  • Operations manager
  • Medical coordinator
  • Robotics engineer
Task Architecture

A better framework than “replace or not replace.”

A strong analysis breaks a role into its true moving parts: information gathering, synthesis, decision preparation, judgment, communication, coordination, exception handling, compliance, and execution. Each of those can have different automation characteristics.

The central question is not whether AI replaces a job. It is how AI rearranges the task stack inside the job.

Augmentation

Where AI accelerates research, drafting, summarization, structuring, and preliminary analysis while humans retain final judgment.

Automation

Where tasks are repetitive, rules are stable, context is accessible, and operational risk is manageable with controls.

Human moat

Where ambiguity, trust, persuasion, accountability, stakeholder alignment, and embodied or contextual judgment remain central.

Future Skill Profiles

The most valuable people will understand both domain work and intelligent systems.

This section can evolve into a major body of work on what future roles will require: workflow literacy, tool supervision, critical evaluation, judgment under uncertainty, domain synthesis, and the ability to work effectively with increasingly agentic systems.
